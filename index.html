Creative Portfolio

Ticketsasa

UI/UX Design

Overview

As part of improving User Experience in the Event space, i chose to revamp the current Ticketsasa app. Ticketsasa is an online ticket provider and it allows it’s customers have the option to purchase tickets through their website and app for a variety of events, such as social events, cooperative activities, etc.

Role

Role :

UI/UX Designer,and conducted User tester.

Team :

Kelvin Musyoka– Solo Project.

Timeline :

3 Weeks.

Tools :

Figma, Pen & Paper.

  • Design Process

    Conceptualization

    Problem Statement & Solution

    My findings revealed that there were no well-known, direct competitors to my app. This meant that the potential for Ticketsasa was huge. However, this was also its biggest risk (i.e. Why wasn’t there another app like this already?). I sought to overcome this risk by seizing the opportunity and revamping the current Ticketsasa App for users to have a much better User Experience.

    My research also helped me shape my problem statement and possible solutions. My original problem statement has remained consistent throughout the app’s development, though the solutions have changed slightly as I’ve progressed.

    Problem Statement

    Ticketsasa App being nested on another app, make it not to be well know unlike the website. Also it’s users need a smooth interactive feel while navigating through the App as from the Login process, event searching, event viewing and purchasing process of the tickets.

    Solution

    Revamping of the current Ticketsasa App that is nested in another App(Pesapal) to be a standalone App and ease the process of navigating through the App as from the starting point until the end. This will make a user be happy and satisfied.

    User Interviews

    I conducted remote user interviews to gain a well-rounded understanding of my users’ needs, goals, and points of friction. I asked questions focused on users' experiences with home renovation. I then analyzed my users' answers by sorting them into an affinity map to gain overarching insights.

    The user research helped me confirm or revise my possible solutions and user stories. From there, I decided to create the app based on my findings.

    Interview Questions:

    1. Have you ever used the App?

    2. What did you like about the app?

    3. What difficulties did you face using the app?

    4. What functions do think should be improved?

    5. What feature(s) would you like to be added?

    6. Did you understand each feature on the prototype?

    User Persona

    Using my findings from the inspiration phase, I created three user personas to represent users with different backgrounds and needs.

    Objectives:

    I asked myself this question before beginning any design processes, keeping the user at the forefront of my thoughts.

    1. What do I really want the user to achieve from this platform?

    2. How’ll it improve their buy ticket process?

    3. What key features would I (Ticketsasa frequent user) want to see in the app?

    4. And how would an average user with very basic knowledge of tech be able to navigate through?

    5. And how useful will this be to them?

    USER FlOW

    In addition to the personas, I also created user journeys and user flows for the app’s main functions. I wanted to understand how my users would complete a function and what they would need to accomplish it.

    SITE MAP

    I then created a site map to organize the structure of my app and have a visual representation of the screens and how they relate to each other.

    LOW-HIGH FIDELITY WIREFRAME

    Next, I started sketching low-fidelity wireframes with pen and paper. I moved on to Figma for my mid- and high-fidelity wireframes. Throughout the different fidelity stages, continuous iterations were made to improve the app’s functionality and UX.

Delicious Apple

Detail

An apple is an edible fruit produced by an apple tree. Apple trees are cultivated worldwide and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus. The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found today.

Apples grown from seed tend to be very different from those of the parents, and the resultant fruit frequently lack desired characteristics. Generally then, apple cultivars are propagated by clonal grafting onto rootstocks. Apple trees grown without rootstocks tend to be larger and much slower to fruit after planting.
